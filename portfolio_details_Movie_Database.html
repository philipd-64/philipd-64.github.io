Rockbuster Stealth Online Launch

SQL analysis and Tableau dashboards to guide movie rental company's transition to online streaming

The Challenge

Rockbuster Stealth LLC, a traditional movie rental company facing competition from streaming services like Netflix and Amazon Prime, needed to launch an online video rental service to remain competitive. The management team required data-driven insights about their existing customer base, rental patterns, and geographic markets to inform their online launch strategy and prioritize where to focus initial efforts.

The Approach

I analyzed Rockbuster's relational database using PostgreSQL, writing SQL queries with table joins to extract rental patterns, customer behavior, and geographic performance metrics. The database contained multiple tables (films, customers, rentals, payments, stores, etc) that needed to be connected through foreign key relationships to answer business questions.

Technical Hurdles & Solutions

Complex Database Relationships: The relational database required joining 5+ tables to answer even basic business questions about customer value and rental patterns. I carefully mapped out the entity relationships and wrote efficient JOIN queries that connected customers → rentals → payments → films, ensuring accurate aggregations without duplicate counting. I then made a very quick interactive map with Tableau to see how many stores are in each location.

The Results

The analysis identified the top 10 customer markets for prioritizing the online launch. Rental pattern analysis showed pricing varied from $0.99 to $4.99 with rental durations between 3-7 days, insights that directly informed the online platform's pricing structure.

The geographic and customer segment insights helped Rockbuster focus their limited launch resources on markets most likely to adopt an online platform, rather than attempting a broad, unfocused rollout.

Project Information

  • Category: Database Analysis
  • Tools: PostgreSQL, Tableau, SQL
  • Techniques: SQL Joins, Data Visualization, Customer Segmentation
  • Date: 2025
  • Context: CareerFoundry Project

Key Findings

Identified top 10 customer markets, revealing geographic concentration of high-value customers that should be prioritized for online launch.

Rental pricing analysis ($0.99-$4.99) and duration patterns (3-7 days) provided concrete benchmarks for structuring the online platform's pricing model and rental terms.

Impact

Geographic and customer insights enabled focused launch strategy rather than resource-intensive broad rollout, concentrating efforts on markets with highest probability of online adoption.
